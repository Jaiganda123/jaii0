Foods in Batangas

Batangas is not only known for its scenic spots and rich culture but also for its delectable cuisine. The province boasts a variety of traditional foods that reflect its vibrant culinary heritage.

Popular Dishes

Bulalo: A hearty beef shank soup with marrow bones, simmered to perfection, known for its rich broth.

Lomi: A thick noodle soup made with egg noodles, pork, liver, and vegetables, topped with a savory sauce.

Tawilis: Freshwater sardines found only in Taal Lake, usually fried until crispy.

Adobong Dilaw: Adobong Dilaw is a pork adobo version that does not use soy sauce. Instead, it uses turmeric which is a type of rhizome that belongs to the ginger family. It is known as “luyang dilaw” in Filipino.

Sinaing na Tulingan: A traditional dish made with bullet tuna slow-cooked in a pot with salt and tamarind leaves.

Gotong Batangas: A savory beef tripe stew flavored with garlic, onions, and spices, unique to Batangas.

Traditional Desserts

Kapeng Barako: A strong coffee variety grown in Batangas, known for its bold and distinct flavor.

Panutsa: A local peanut brittle made from caramelized sugar and whole peanuts.

Suman: A rice cake wrapped in banana leaves, often served with sugar or latik (coconut caramel).

Ube Halaya: A sweet dessert made from purple yam, milk, and sugar, often topped with latik.

Bibingka: A type of rice cake traditionally cooked in clay pots, topped with salted eggs and cheese.

Kalamay: A sticky sweet rice cake made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar.
